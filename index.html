    Transit

    Getting to/from NYC

    3 major airports serve NYC: LaGuardia (LGA), JFK and Newark (EWR).

    LGA is closest to the Brooklyn Marriott. There are several options to get to and from LGA. The quickest and easiest is to take a cab (about $30). All cabs take credit cards, though it is always good to carry extra cash just in case. Please note: most cabs will only take 4 passengers/fare.


    Larger Map

    JFK is further away, but still a good option. JFK is serviced by the A train line. The airtrain from all terminals to the A train is $5/fare. Once at the A train subway fares are $2.50 for a single ride or we recommend purchasing a Pay-per-ride subway card ($2.25/ride). Then, take the A train to Jay Street/Metrotech station.


    Larger Map

    EWR is in New Jersey so we do not recommend taking a taxi to/from the airport. It will be very expensive. Public transit options, however, are easy to use, inexpensive and efficient. Airtrain services all terminals at EWR and connects passengers to the NJTransit station. Fares are $12-$14/one way. Take NJT to New York Penn Station (**not to be confused with Newark Penn Station!). At NY Penn follow signs to the 2/3 train going towards Brooklyn. Take Brooklyn-bound 3 train to Borough Hall stop.

    First time in Brooklyn? Here is a list of some of our favorite places to walk, eat, shop and visit.

    Downtown Brooklyn area

    The Brooklyn Bridge: Walking across the Brooklyn Bridge is a great experience (just remember to stay left because the bicyclists are quite agressive). See the Manhattan skyline and marvel at the engineering and beauty of the country’s first suspension bridge.

    Brooklyn Bridge Park: A great spot on a warm spring day, great views of lower manhattan, the bridges, the east river and Lady Liberty in the distance. brooklynbridgepark.org

    The Brooklyn Ice Cream Factory: located on Fulton Pier right next to the Brooklyn Bridge Park. This is a great place to stop for a cone after an afternoon in the park. Or after a (pizza) pie at Grimaldi’s (if you can handle it). brooklynicecreamfactory.com

    Grimaldi’s Coal Brick-Oven Pizza: Fans of this New York institution include the late, great Frank Sinatra and former NYC mayor Rudy Guiliani. Don’t be intimidated by the line, it goes quick and is well worth the wait. grimaldisnyc.com

    Jacques Torres Chocolate: Buy bon-bons like Pistachio Marzipan, Earl Grey and Champagne Truffle for later or get the iced hot chocolate for now. mrchocolate.com

    Smith and Carroll Street Shopping and Eating: Apparently someone’s given this area the ridiculous name Bococa a mashup of Boerum Hill, Cobble Hill and Carroll Gardens the neighborhoods that make up this shopping district. This is a fun place to poke around and get cool Brooklyn gear to bring home to friends and family. Here’s a link with maps and more info: brooklynnow.com

    For Art(s) Lovers!

    BAM (Brooklyn Academy of Music): this performance arts space has more than just music, it also has dance, theater, opera, talks (with actors, artists, and writers like Junot Diaz and Richard Russo) and film. If you’re looking for something to do Saturday evening look no further. This place has everything. bam.org

    Brooklyn Museum of Art: This museum has the second largest art collection in NYC after The Met. Well worth a visit and right next door to the Brooklyn Botanic Gardens. brooklynmuseum.org

    New York City Transit Museum: Not exactly art but this place is fun and within walking distance to the Brooklyn Marriott. Located in a defunct subway station, you can see exhibits on how the subway system was planned and built and get inside some original subway cars. Please keep in mind that this is a favorite spot for parents to take their kids and the subway tiles make for some intense acoustics. Prepare for a noisy visit. mta.info

    Food and Booze

    Juniors: Home of Brooklyn’s famous cheesecake, this restaurant features a New York Deli style menu and some incredible cocktail names, like ‘Thug Passion’. There are many, many better options for a meal in Brooklyn, but Junior’s is definitely a fun tourist destination. juniorscheescake.com

    Close to the hotel there are a bunch of good spots to eat, here’s a list of some places you can walk to: Henry St. Ale House, Henry’s End, Waterfront Ale House, Yemen Cafe and Restaurant.

    A little farther afield (a short subway or cab ride away) but worth it: Olea, Flatbush Farm (If you come here for brunch do NOT get the farmer’s breakfast you will be completely unsatisfied. Otherwise it’s good), James, Berlyn, and Frank's Cocktail Lounge (site of our first date and favorite bar).
